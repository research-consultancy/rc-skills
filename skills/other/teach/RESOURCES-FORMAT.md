# RESOURCES.md Format

`RESOURCES.md` is the curated set of trusted sources for this topic. Ground explanations in these sources rather than parametric guesses. Develop judgment through the collaborators and research communities listed here.

## Structure

```md
# {Topic} Resources

## Knowledge and standards

- [Cochrane Handbook for Systematic Reviews of Interventions](https://training.cochrane.org/handbook)
  Authoritative methods guidance. Use for: review design, bias assessment, synthesis, and interpretation.
- [PRISMA 2020 statement](https://www.prisma-statement.org/)
  Reporting standard for systematic reviews. Use for: protocol-to-manuscript reporting checks.
- [PubMed Help](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/help/)
  Official search syntax and behavior. Use for: constructing and debugging reproducible PubMed searches.

## Practice and judgment

- Local: institutional information specialist or medical librarian
  Use for: peer review of systematic-search concepts, syntax, and database coverage.
- Local: biostatistics methods meeting or journal club
  Use for: critique of estimands, model assumptions, bias, and interpretation.
```

## Rules

- **High-trust only.** Prefer primary studies, methods papers, reporting guidelines, official documentation, and recognized methodological organizations. Record whether guidance is normative, empirical, or expert consensus.
- **Annotate every entry.** A bare link is useless in three months. Add one line: what it covers and when to reach for it.
- **Group by Knowledge and standards / Practice and judgment.** It is fine for a resource to appear in only one group.
- **Surface gaps explicitly.** If no good resource exists for an area the mission needs, write a `## Gaps` section listing what is missing. This drives future search.
- **Prune ruthlessly.** A resource that turned out to be wrong, shallow, or off-mission should be removed, not buried. Better five sharp sources than thirty mediocre ones.
- **Record collaboration preferences.** If the user cannot access a librarian, statistician, clinical collaborator, or research community, record that constraint so future lessons offer realistic alternatives.

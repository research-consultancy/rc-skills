Empagliflozin versus placebo in adults with heart failure with reduced ejection fraction.

Smith J(1), Khan A(2).

Author information:
(1)Example University.
(2)Example Hospital.

BACKGROUND: The benefit of empagliflozin in adults with heart failure with reduced
ejection fraction remains under study.
METHODS: Adults were randomized to empagliflozin or placebo and followed for heart
failure hospitalization.
RESULTS: Empagliflozin reduced heart failure hospitalization.

Adherence to guideline-directed therapy in adults with heart failure with reduced ejection fraction.

Nguyen E(1).

Author information:
(1)Example Heart Institute.

BACKGROUND: Contemporary therapy for HFrEF may include SGLT2 inhibitors.
METHODS: We measured prescription rates for beta-blockers and mineralocorticoid
receptor antagonists in an outpatient registry. SGLT2 treatment and outcomes were not
assessed.
RESULTS: Adherence to beta-blockers remained suboptimal.

Short-term ventricular remodeling after dapagliflozin initiation in HFrEF.

Wilson F(1).

Author information:
(1)Example Cardiology Centre.

METHODS: In this prospective single-arm study, adults with HFrEF received
dapagliflozin for twelve weeks. Echocardiography was performed before and after
treatment. No untreated or active comparator group was enrolled.
RESULTS: Left ventricular volume decreased after treatment.

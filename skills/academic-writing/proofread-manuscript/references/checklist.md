# Medical Manuscript Proofreading Checklist

Apply every relevant check. Record each as passed, failed, or uncheckable.

## Scientific and numerical integrity

- Recalculate percentages from counts, counts from percentages, totals, subgroup sums, flow diagrams, and other derived values.
- Trace every key estimate, interval, p-value, event count, sample size, and denominator across abstract, text, tables, figures, supplements, and analysis output.
- Check point estimate–interval–p-value consistency, null values, effect direction, units, impossible values, follow-up windows, and one rounding policy.
- Verify that each rate names the correct risk set. Distinguish admission-based outcomes from discharge-based and post-discharge outcomes.
- Map every score, category, or risk band with open/closed endpoints; check complete, non-overlapping coverage and consistent transforms.
- Reconcile participant flow, exclusions, analyzed populations, and duplicate or overlapping cohorts.
- Compare every reported result with supplied analysis output and quote source versus manuscript values for discrepancies.
- Check that causal language, conclusions, and recommendations remain within the study design and data.

## Language and structure

- Correct grammar, syntax, punctuation, spelling, agreement, articles, parallelism, tense, and voice where they affect meaning.
- Resolve ambiguity, dangling modifiers, inconsistent terminology, unexplained jargon, accidental repetition, and unsupported superlatives.
- Define abbreviations at first use, use one canonical form, and expand every table-specific abbreviation in that table's footnote.
- Preserve distinctions between association and causation, statistical and clinical significance, absence of evidence and evidence of absence.
- Align the title, abstract, methods, results, discussion, conclusion, tables, figures, and supplement.
- Confirm that each outcome, time point, population, model, and effect measure is named precisely.

## References and claim support

- Match every in-text citation to one reference-list entry and every listed reference to an in-text citation.
- Verify each primary finding, guideline statement, and mechanism claim against the cited PubMed record and accessible primary full text.
- Check author, title, journal, year, volume, pages, PMID, DOI, publication type, correction, and retraction status.
- Identify duplicates, numbering drift, wrong-paper attribution, and reviews cited as though they supplied primary data.
- Confirm the target journal's current reference style and count limit.

## Figure–text concordance

- Match every higher/lower, increase/decrease, magnitude, trend, ranking, and time-series statement to the plotted or tabulated data.
- Check that every figure and table is numbered, called out in order, and described consistently everywhere.
- Make every title and legend self-contained: population, quantity, unit, denominator, symbols, error bars, markers, reference lines, shaded regions, and abbreviations.
- Label and center both axes; use legible ticks, consistent panel labels, comparable scales, and distinguishable colors or line styles.
- Keep units with variables rather than repeated in cells; align values and use consistent significant digits within each variable.
- Inspect rendered layout for clipping, wrapping, crowding, unreadable labels, and run-together values.

## Journal and submission package

- Retrieve the target journal's current official instructions and compare exact word limits, abstract structure, headings, keywords, reference limits, declarations, and table/figure file requirements.
- Match the exact manuscript title and journal name across manuscript, cover letter, response letter, and submission metadata.
- Verify the current cover-letter date and the editor's current name, title, spelling, and honorific from an official source.
- Match author names, initials, degrees, order, affiliations, superscripts, acknowledgments, and corresponding-author details across files.
- Verify ethics/IRB, consent, registration, funding, conflicts, author contributions, acknowledgments, and data/code availability statements.

## Revision-package promises

- Map every reviewer/editor comment to a real manuscript change or an explicit reasoned rebuttal.
- Locate every claimed addition, deletion, limitation, statistic, analysis, figure element, footnote, and citation in the current files.
- Compare quoted revised text with the manuscript verbatim, including statistics and citation numbers.
- Check that response-letter citation numbers still resolve to the intended final references.

## Hidden document state

- Inspect DOCX OOXML for tracked insertions, deletions, comments, hidden text, and stale metadata in the manuscript, supplement, and cover letter.
- Inspect PDFs for annotation and comment layers.
- Report every leftover tracked change or comment in a file presented as clean as Critical, naming the file and quoting the affected content.
